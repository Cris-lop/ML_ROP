While traditional methods for drilling optimization exist, they are often time-consuming and have significant limitations.     <section id="methodology">

        <div class="container">

            <h2>Methodology & Technologies</h2>

            <p>The project followed a structured machine learning workflow, beginning with the collection and pre-processing of data from multiple wells. Key technologies and methods include:</p>

            <ul>

                <li><strong>Machine Learning Models:</strong> Gradient Boosting Regressor (GBR) and XGBoost were developed to predict ROP.</li>

                <li><strong>Optimization Algorithm:</strong> Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) was used to find the optimal set of controllable drilling parameters.</li>

                <li><strong>Benchmarking:</strong> The performance of the ML models was rigorously compared against traditional engineering models, specifically Maurer's and Burgoyne & Young's equations.</li>

                <li><strong>Feature Analysis:</strong> Techniques like Spearman Correlation and Permutation Feature Importance were used to understand the relationships between different drilling parameters and ROP.</li>

            </ul>

        </div>

    </section>
